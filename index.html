About Me

I work within a public sector Digital, Data and Technology environment providing user and application support. My role focuses on reliable service delivery, security, collaboration, and continuous improvement. This site supports my End Point Assessment professional discussion.

This portfolio is structured around key KSB areas that are discussed during the EPA, with each tab containing evidence and reflection aligned to assessment criteria.

Organisational Culture

I work within a local authority DDaT environment where governance, security, and accountability introduce necessary constraints. These controls ensure public trust while still allowing innovation within defined frameworks.

I focus on outcome-driven work rather than activity-driven metrics by addressing root causes and improving services, not just closing tickets. I also adapt my communication style when working with external suppliers or teams that operate under different cultural pressures.

Data Ethics

My role requires regular handling of personal and sensitive information. I comply with UK GDPR by applying least-privilege access, verifying user identity, and ensuring data is only accessed for legitimate purposes.

For testing and troubleshooting, I avoid live user data and use anonymised or dummy accounts. If I discovered a potential data breach or security vulnerability, I would contain the issue immediately and follow formal council escalation and reporting procedures.

Problem Solving

I use structured problem-solving approaches such as root cause analysis, the 5 Whys, and collaborative swarming to diagnose incidents accurately and prevent repeat issues.

I regularly use whiteboarding to map systems, authentication flows, and dependencies, ensuring the *right* problem is identified before implementing a fix.
